### **1. Collaborating Effectively with Developers (20 minutes)**

- **What Developers Focus On When Reviewing a Design Document:**
    - **Clarity**: “How exactly is this system supposed to function?”
    - **Exception Handling**: “What should happen in edge cases or unexpected situations?”
    - **Data Structure**: “How can this be implemented in code?”
        
- **How to Structure a Design Document Developers Can Work With Immediately:**
    - **Must-Have Elements**:
        - **Feature Description**: What the system does from a user’s perspective
        - **Inputs/Outputs**: What users input and what the system returns
        - **Flowchart**: Step-by-step flow of how the feature behaves
        - **UI Wireframes**: Visuals of how and where this feature appears on screen
            
- **Example: Inventory System Design Document (Simplified)**
    - **Feature Description**: Players can pick up, equip, and discard items.
    - **Constraints**: Inventory holds a maximum of 20 items.
    - **Exception Handling**: When inventory is full, new items cannot be picked up, and a message is shown to the player.
    - **Data Structure**:  
        `ItemID`, `ItemName`, `ItemType`, `Quantity`

➡️ **Designing with these details helps developers quickly understand and implement your designs.**

### **2. Collaborating Effectively with Other Designers (20 minutes)**

- **What Fellow Designers Need From Your Design Document:**
    - **Visual References**: What kind of mood, style, or theme should the UI/character/environment convey?
    - **Screen Flow Diagrams**: How the user moves between screens step by step
    - **Interaction Details**: What kind of animation or response is triggered by user actions (e.g., button presses)

➡️ **Providing this level of detail enables designers to stay aligned and produce consistent, high-quality visual results.**
